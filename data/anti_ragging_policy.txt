Anti-Ragging Policy
College of Engineering - Zero Tolerance

1. Definition of Ragging
   Ragging includes any act that causes physical or psychological harm, fear, or embarrassment
   to a student. This includes but is not limited to:
   - Forcing a student to perform tasks against their will.
   - Verbal abuse, bullying, or intimidation.
   - Any form of physical violence or threat of violence.
   - Forcing juniors to address seniors in a particular way.
   - Any act that affects the mental health or dignity of a student.

2. Punishments
   Depending on the severity of the offense:
   - Warning and counseling (first minor offense).
   - Suspension from classes for 1-4 weeks.
   - Withholding results or scholarship.
   - Debarment from examinations.
   - Expulsion from hostel.
   - Rustication from the college.
   - FIR with local police under UGC Anti-Ragging Regulations.

3. Reporting Ragging
   - Anti-Ragging Helpline: 1800-180-5522 (24/7, toll-free)
   - College Anti-Ragging Committee Email: antiragging@college.edu
   - Anonymous complaint box outside the Dean's office (Block A, 1st Floor).
   - Online complaint form on the college website under "Student Grievances".
   - WhatsApp helpline: +91-98765-43210

4. Anti-Ragging Committee
   - Chairperson: Dr. Meera Kapoor (Dean of Students)
   - Members: 3 faculty members, 2 senior students, 1 parent representative
   - The committee investigates all complaints within 7 working days.
   - The identity of the complainant is kept strictly confidential.

5. Mandatory Declaration
   - Every student must sign an anti-ragging affidavit at the time of admission.
   - Parents/guardians must also sign a declaration.
   - Affidavits are submitted online at antiragging.in as per UGC/AICTE norms.

6. Awareness Programs
   - A mandatory anti-ragging orientation is conducted during Freshers' Week.
   - Mentorship program: Each fresher is assigned a senior student mentor.
   - Monthly awareness sessions by the counseling cell.
