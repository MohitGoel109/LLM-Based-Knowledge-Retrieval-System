2. Placement Process
   The university follows a four-stage campus recruitment process:
   Step 1: Pre-Placement Talk (PPT) — Companies present their profile, job roles, and expectations
   Step 2: Written Test — Aptitude, technical, and domain-specific assessments
   Step 3: Group Discussion — Evaluation of communication, teamwork, and leadership skills
   Step 4: Personal Interview — Technical and HR interviews

3. Career Development Centre (CDC)
   The CDC provides comprehensive career support services:
   - Resume building guidance
   - Mock interviews
   - Group discussion training
   - Soft skills and communication workshops
   - Sector-specific training programs

   Objective: To provide the appropriate career platform for budding talent to flourish.

4. Placement Timeline
   - Placement drives commence in September every year
   - Orientation sessions are conducted before the recruitment season begins
   - The CDC facilitates year-round interactions with industry partners

5. Industry Engagement
   The university has signed MOUs with renowned companies and institutions across diverse sectors:
   - Training: Practical skill application in real-world problem-solving
   - Projects: Project-based learning addressing industry-specific challenges
   - Corporate Life: In-house training providing hands-on experience under mentor supervision
